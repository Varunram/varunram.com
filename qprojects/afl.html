American Fuzzy Lop

American Fuzzy Lop or AFL was developed at google by lcamtuf and later open sourced. It is a dumb, mutation based fuzzer which randomly mutates inputs into the source program. It needs to be compiled along with the program executable (eg the gcc compiler) and then one can proceed with fuzzing the program.

To fuzz the program, one must run the target executable from within afl using aflfuzz and then wait for it to discover paths. There's a UI attached which shows the current state of fuzzing since complete fuzzing may take several hours if not days on standard consumer hardware.

The project I worked on at École Normale Supérieure, Paris was regarding the applicability of fuzzers to LaTeX and to see if the LaTeX executable was vulnerable to rogue pdfs and inputs. During the course of two months, we started off from the basics of fuzzers, moved on to implement one test case after the other feeding inputs to afl, compiled ouputs and then finally documented our findings.
