American Fuzzy Lop

American Fuzzy Lop or AFL was developed at google by lcamtuf and later open sourced. It is a dumb, mutation based fuzzer which randomly mutates inputs to the source program. AFL needs to be compiled along with the program executable (eg. the gcc compiler) for one to proceed with fuzzing the program.

To fuzz the program, one must pass the target executable to aflfuzz and wait for it to discover paths. There's a command line UI attached to aflfuzz which shows the current state of fuzzing. Complete fuzzing may take several hours if not days on standard consumer hardware and this is a main motivation behind having the CLI UI.

The project I worked on at École Normale Supérieure, Paris was regarding the applicability of fuzzers to LaTeX and to see if the LaTeX executable was vulnerable to rogue pdfs and inputs. During the course of two months, we started off from the basics of fuzzers, moved on to implement one test case after the other feeding inputs to afl, compiled ouputs and then finally documented our findings. We also experimented with other fuzzers to compare their performance to afl and see where we could improve afl.
