Varunram Ganesh is a Research Engineer and Entrepreneur at the MIT Digital Currency Initiative (DCI)

His background is in technology research, data privacy and product strategy, and his interests span cryptocurrencies, personal data security and emerging technologies.

At the MIT DCI, Varunram works on a blockchain platform that enables investments in climate change mitigation projects, and looks into how blockchains can be used to provide control and transparency over personal data

As an active entrepreneur, he performs research on the intersection of data privacy and student inclusion at Kratos, a startup focused on student data privacy, and leads Bitcoin education initiatives at BitHyve

In the past, Varunram was part of the Yale Open Innovation Lab, a member of the founding team at Musicoin, and worked on the Bitcoin Lightning Network at the MIT DCI.

In his spare time, he loves to play arcade games and watch horror movies.
